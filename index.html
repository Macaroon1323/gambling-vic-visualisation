Gambling in Victoria: Regional Trends and Impacts (2011–2025)

This visualisation explores electronic gaming machine (EGM) gambling trends across Victoria, analysing expenditure, density, and social impact across local government areas (LGAs). Data is drawn from the Victorian Gambling and Casino Control Commission (VGCCC) and related public datasets to reveal patterns in gambling behaviour over time.

Gambling Expenditure Across Victoria (2024)

Regions with the highest gambling expenditure are primarily concentrated in Melbourne’s outer metropolitan areas—particularly Brimbank, Casey, and Greater Dandenong—reflecting the clustering of venues in areas of socioeconomic disadvantage.

EGM Density vs Expenditure per Adult (2015–2024)

This scatter plot compares expenditure against electronic gaming machine (EGM) density per 1,000 adults across Victorian LGAs. The interactive slider reveals how these metrics evolve over time, showing consistent high densities in metropolitan areas with emerging trends in regional centres.

Long-Term Expenditure Trends

Over the past decade, gambling expenditure in Victoria has experienced periods of decline due to policy changes and pandemic-related closures. However, post-2020 recovery has seen a resurgence, with total losses now exceeding pre-pandemic levels. The upward trajectory reflects the enduring prevalence of EGMs in local entertainment economies.

Top 10 LGAs by Player Loss (Sept 2025)

Gambling losses remain heavily concentrated in Melbourne’s outer suburban LGAs. Casey, Hume, Brimbank, and Greater Dandenong consistently rank among the highest, driven by high population and venue density. This ranking highlights the geographic concentration of player losses across the state.

Alcohol and Gambling Correlation

Trends in gambling expenditure often parallel alcohol-related hospitalisations, revealing shared social and economic pressures. Both indicators have increased across the last decade, suggesting overlapping public health challenges in communities with high-risk gambling and alcohol consumption patterns.
